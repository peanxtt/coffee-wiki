A Brief History

The story of coffee begins in the ancient coffee forests of the Ethiopian plateau. Legend says the goat herder Kaldi first discovered the potential of these beloved beans. From there, coffee cultivation and trade began on the Arabian Peninsula.

By the 15th century, coffee was being grown in the Yemeni district of Arabia and by the 16th century it was known in Persia, Egypt, Syria, and Turkey. It wasn't until the 17th century that coffee made its way to Europe and became popular across the continent.

Key Milestones

850 CE Discovery in Ethiopia by Kaldi
1475 First coffee shop opens in Constantinople
1600s Coffee enters Europe via Venice
