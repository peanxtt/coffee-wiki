Matcha (抹茶)

Matcha is a finely ground powder of specially grown and processed green tea leaves, traditionally consumed in East Asia. Unlike other teas where leaves are steeped, matcha involves consuming the entire leaf, providing a rich, full-bodied umami flavor and a potent concentration of nutrients.

difference Distinctions

vs. Coffee
Contains L-theanine which promotes relaxation without drowsiness, avoiding the "jittery" spike of coffee.
vs. Sencha
Shaded for 3-4 weeks before harvest (Tencha) to boost chlorophyll, whereas standard Sencha is sun-grown.

The Importance of Shading

The defining characteristic of matcha is the shading process (Oishita) which lasts for 20-30 days before harvest. This blocks sunlight, forcing the plant to overproduce chlorophyll and amino acids, specifically L-Theanine, which creates the unique savory "umami" taste.
