menu_book Region Summary

Sidamo (or Sidama) is a massive territory in the southern highlands of Ethiopia, often considered the birthplace of coffee alongside the neighboring Kaffa region. The region's coffee is highly prized in the specialty market for its complexity. The high elevation, generous rainfall, and rich volcanic soil create the ideal conditions for growing high-quality Arabica.

Coffees from Sidamo are typically processed using both washed and natural methods. Washed Sidamos are known for their elegance, presenting tea-like characteristics with distinct lemon and floral notes. Natural Sidamos, on the other hand, tend to be fruit-forward, with heavy blueberry or strawberry notes and a syrupy body.

Terroir & Production

  • check_circle Garden Coffee: Most coffee here is grown by smallholders in "garden" plots alongside other crops like Enset (false banana).
  • check_circle Genetic Diversity: The region is home to thousands of indigenous "landrace" varieties, often grouped simply as "Heirloom".
  • check_circle Cooperative Model: Farmers typically deliver cherries to centralized washing stations organized by cooperatives.
