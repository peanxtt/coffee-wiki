Coffee Bean Varieties

While there are over 100 species of coffee plants, the global coffee industry is dominated by four main varieties: Arabica, Robusta, Liberica, and Excelsa. Each offers a distinct profile, from the delicate acidity of Arabica to the smoky depth of Liberica.

grain The Four Major Varieties

Arabica

Coffea arabica

~60-70% Global Production

Known for its smooth, complex flavor profile and lack of bitterness. Arabica beans are fragile and require specific high-altitude conditions to thrive. They are the standard for specialty coffee.

Flavor Profile Sweet, Fruit, Berries, Sugar
Caffeine Low (1.2% - 1.5%)
Growing Conditions High Alt (600m+), Cool
Primary Use Pour-over, Espresso

Robusta

Coffea canephora

~30-40% Global Production

A hardier plant with higher caffeine content and a stronger, harsher taste profile. Often described as earthy or rubbery, but valued for its deep body and rich crema in espresso blends.

Flavor Profile Earthy, Nutty, Bitter, Grain
Caffeine High (2.2% - 2.7%)
Growing Conditions Low Alt, Hot/Humid
Primary Use Instant, Espresso Blends

Liberica

Coffea liberica

< 2% Global Production

Rare and unique, known for its irregular bean shape and a polarizing profile that is smoky, nutty, and floral. Primarily grown in the Philippines (known as Kapeng Barako).

Flavor Profile Smoky, Woody, Floral
Caffeine Very Low
Growing Conditions Specific Humidity
Primary Use Heritage Brews

Excelsa

Coffea liberica var. dewevrei

< 1% Global Production

Historically classified separately, now considered a variety of Liberica. It grows on large trees and contributes a tart, fruity, dark-roast character to blends, adding complexity.

Flavor Profile Tart, Fruity, Dark
Caffeine Medium
Growing Conditions Medium Alt, Southeast Asia
Primary Use Blends for depth
