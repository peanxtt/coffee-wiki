Caffè Latte

A mild, milky coffee drink consisting of one or more shots of espresso, served with a large amount of steamed milk and a thin layer of microfoam. The name comes from the Italian caffè e latte, meaning "coffee and milk".

Serving Size 240–350 ml
Calories ~100–190 kcal
Ratio 1:3

grocery Ingredients

  • water_drop

    Espresso

    1-2 shots (30-60ml), preferably a medium-dark roast.

  • science

    Steamed Milk

    8-10 oz (240-300ml) steamed to 60–65°C.

  • cloud

    Microfoam

    Thin layer (~1cm) of smooth, silky foam on top.

Step-by-Step Preparation

Step 01

Prepare Espresso

Grind 18-20g of coffee beans fine. Pull a double shot of espresso into a wide ceramic cup.

Step 02

Steam Milk

Purge the steam wand. Steam milk while incorporating a small amount of air to create microfoam. Aim for 60°C (140°F).

Step 03

Texture & Swirl

Tap the pitcher to break large bubbles. Swirl vigorously to incorporate the foam into the liquid milk for a glossy texture.

Step 04

The Pour

Tilt the cup. Pour the milk into the center of the espresso from a slight height, then lower the pitcher to create latte art.

public Variations by Region

Café au Lait

France

Made with strong brewed coffee (usually French Press) instead of espresso, and hot milk.

Spanish Latte

Spain

A standard latte with added sweetened condensed milk for a richer, sweeter flavor.

Iced Latte

Global

Cold milk and ice served with espresso poured over the top. No steaming required.
