Understanding Matcha Grades

Not all powdered green tea is created equal. From the vibrant, umami-rich ceremonial blends of Uji to robust culinary grades for baking, understanding the grading system is key to your perfect cup.

Top Tier workspace_premium

Ceremonial Grade

The highest quality matcha, made from the youngest tea leaves with stems and veins removed. Intended to be whisked with hot water and enjoyed pure.

Vibrant Electric Green

High-quality tea often from the second harvest. It has a slightly bolder flavor that pairs perfectly with milk and sweeteners.

Culinary Grade

Made from older leaves lower on the stem. Its robust, bitter profile is designed to shine through flour, sugar, and fats in cooking.

What Determines the Grade?

It's not just marketing. The grade is scientifically determined by cultivation methods that alter the chemical composition of the leaf.

wb_twilight

Shading Duration

Ceremonial grade is shade-grown for 3-4 weeks. This inhibits photosynthesis, boosting L-Theanine and chlorophyll for sweetness and color.

spa

Leaf Selection

Only the top, youngest two leaves are plucked for ceremonial grade. Older leaves lower on the stem are tougher and more bitter, reserved for culinary use.

scatter_plot

Processing & Grinding

Stems and veins are removed (Tencha) before being stone-ground into a micron-fine powder. Faster, heat-generating machine grinding is used for lower grades.
