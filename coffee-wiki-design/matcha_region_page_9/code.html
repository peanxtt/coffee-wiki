Usucha (Thin Tea)

The standard ceremonial preparation of matcha.

Usucha (薄茶) is the most common way to prepare ceremonial grade matcha. Characterized by a lighter, frothy consistency compared to Koicha (thick tea), it balances the vegetal umami of the tea leaves with a refreshing, clean finish. It is the everyday style of tea ceremony consumption.

Ingredients List

Ceremonial Grade Matcha 2g (2 scoops)
Hot Water (70°C - 80°C) 70ml

* Note: Sifting the matcha powder beforehand is crucial to avoid clumps and ensure a smooth foam.

blender

Step-by-Step Preparation

1. Warm the Vessel

Pour hot water into your chawan (tea bowl) to warm it up. Soak the whisk (chasen) to soften the bamboo tines.

1

2. Measure & Sift

Discard the warming water and dry the bowl. Place the sifter over the bowl and measure 2 scoops (chashaku) of matcha, pressing it through the sieve.

2

3. Add Water

Pour about 70ml of hot water (not boiling, ideally 80°C/176°F) gently onto the powder.

3

4. Whisk

Using the whisk, mix rapidly in a 'W' motion using your wrist. Whisk until a fine, creamy foam covers the surface. Lift the whisk slowly from the center.

4

public Regional Variations

Urasenke School

Prefers a rich layer of fine foam covering the entire surface of the tea.

Omotesenke School

Prefers only a partial foam (often a 'crescent moon' shape), leaving a pond of deep green tea visible.

Pro Tip

For the best foam, move your wrist back and forth, not your arm. The motion should be fast and vigorous, creating fine micro-bubbles.
