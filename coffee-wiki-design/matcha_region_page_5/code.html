Region Overview

Uji (宇治) is not merely a location but a standard of quality in the world of Japanese green tea. Located just south of Kyoto city, the region benefits from a unique microclimate created by the Uji River. The warm days and cool nights produce morning mists that naturally shade the tea bushes, slowing their growth and concentrating chlorophyll and amino acids.

Historically, Uji is the birthplace of the Sohen (shading) method developed in the 16th century, which evolved into the modern canopy shading techniques used for Gyokuro and Matcha. The soil is nutrient-rich and well-drained, contributing to the distinct "oika" (covering scent) and powerful savory profile known as umami.

Terroir Characteristics

  • check_circle River Mists: Natural moisture regulation prevents frost and harsh direct sunlight.
  • check_circle Fertile Soil: Ancient river sediment provides excellent drainage and mineral content.
  • check_circle Tradition: Centuries of generational knowledge in shading and hand-picking (Tezumi).

Matcha from Uji is typically characterized by a lack of astringency, a creamy mouthfeel, and a sophisticated, multi-layered flavor profile that balances sweetness with vegetal depth.
