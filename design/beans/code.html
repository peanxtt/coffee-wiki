The Triad of Species

While the commercial coffee market is dominated by two primary species, the genus Coffea comprises over 120 identified species. Our focus centers on the three pillars of cultivation: Arabica, known for its complexity; Robusta, for its vigor; and Liberica, for its distinct, bold presence.

"To understand the roast, one must first respect the lineage. A bean's soul is written in its genetic sequence long before it meets the fire."
— Senior Curator, Margaret Van Der Rohe

Typica

The ancestral variety of the Arabica family tree. Low yield but exceptional cup quality, characterized by a clean, sweet acidity.

The most sought-after cultivar in specialty coffee. It exhibits tea-like qualities with extraordinary floral notes of jasmine and bergamot.

Ideal Temperature

Arabica thrives between 15°C and 24°C. Excessive heat causes the fruit to ripen too quickly, leading to a loss of complexity and potential bean damage.

Rainfall Patterns

An annual rainfall of 1,500 to 2,500 mm is required, with a distinct dry season to trigger flowering and ensure a uniform harvest.
